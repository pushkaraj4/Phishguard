A phishing website is a fraudulent website designed to deceive users into believing it is a legitimate site. Its purpose is to steal sensitive information, such as login credentials, personal details, or financial data.

Phishing websites work by mimicking the appearance and functionality of legitimate websites. They often use deceptive emails or messages to lure users in, where they are prompted to enter sensitive information. This information is then collected by cybercriminals for malicious purposes.

Phishing websites can lead to identity theft, financial losses, and other serious consequences. Falling victim to a phishing attack can result in compromised personal and financial security.

Phishing websites often exhibit red flags such as misspelled domain names, suspicious URLs, unsecured connections, and requests for sensitive information via email. Users should verify the website's legitimacy before sharing any personal data.

If you suspect a website is a phishing site, do not enter any personal information. Close the website, report it to the appropriate authorities, and notify the legitimate entity it impersonates.
